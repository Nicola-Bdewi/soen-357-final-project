Espresso

Espresso is made purely from coffee beans, which are naturally gluten-free. The process of making espresso involves forcing a small amount of nearly boiling water under pressure through finely-ground coffee beans. There are no additives or flavorings in a standard espresso, making it a safe choice for those with gluten intolerance.

Cappuccino

A traditional cappuccino consists of 3 layers: espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. The espresso part is gluten-free. The milk used can be replaced with lactose-free milk or non-dairy alternatives like almond, soy, or oat milk for those who are lactose intolerant. The foam is also made from the same milk or milk alternative, making the whole drink lactose-free.

Flat White

A flat white also contains espresso and a smaller amount of steamed milk. The key here is that there are no additional flavorings or additives, so the drink is naturally free of tree nuts. It's important to note that if a milk alternative made from tree nuts (like almond milk) is used, then this would not be a safe option for individuals with tree nut allergies.

Bagel

Bagels are typically made from wheat flour, water, yeast, and salt. However, for those with a wheat allergy or gluten intolerance, bagels can be made from a variety of gluten-free flours such as rice, corn, or quinoa flour. These flours do not contain the gluten protein that causes reactions in those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease.
